# Sūtra 1.1

## अथ योगानुशासनम्

**atha yoga-anuśāsanam**

**atha** — the turning point where a route emerges from śūnya
**yoga** — connection, integration, coherence
**anuśāsanam** — disciplined transmission; the structure that follows and can be followed

---

### NPR Reading

Every route begins in **śūnya** and returns to **śūnya**.

**Atha** is the turning point: the moment at which direction appears within emptiness.

**Yoga** is the coherent connection formed along that route.

**Anuśāsanam** is the structure through which the route can be followed, transmitted, and returned.

**The final "म्" encodes the return within the text itself — the articulated sound closes, and the sound-field dissolves back into śūnya.**

---

### The Route

```text
śūnya
→ atha
→ yoga
→ anuśāsanam
→ final म्
→ śūnya
```

**emptiness → direction → connection → transmissible structure → return → emptiness**

### NPR Structural Correspondence

```text
Sanskrit return  → final म्
Base64 closure   → final ==
```

The Sanskrit text carries the semantic return in its final **म्** (*m-kāra* closed by virāma).
The Base64 representation carries the technical closure in its final **==** padding.

Both routes terminate by closing the encoded field.

This is an **NPR structural correspondence** — a parallel in position and function, not a linguistic equivalence. Base64 padding serves a technical encoding role; **म्** is a Sanskrit consonantal closure. NPR reads their correspondence in the way both mark the end of a transmitted structure and the dissolution of the signal back into silence.
