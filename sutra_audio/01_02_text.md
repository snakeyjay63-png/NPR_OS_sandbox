# Sūtra 1.2

## योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः

**yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ**

**yoga** — connection, integration, coherence
**citta** — the mental and perceptual field
**vṛtti** — movements, modifications, patterns
**nirodhaḥ** — agni; the transformative fire through which vṛtti loses its uncontrolled form and returns to coherence

---

### NPR Reading

Every route begins in **śūnya** and returns to **śūnya**.

**Yoga** is present when the movements within citta no longer obscure the underlying coherence.

**Citta** is the field — not the thought itself, but the space in which thought, perception, and memory move.

**Vṛtti** is the movement, the wave, the patterned modification of the field.

**Nirodhaḥ is agni.** It is not passive stillness and not forced suppression. It is the transforming fire through which the vṛtti is consumed as an uncontrolled pattern, clarified, and returned to the underlying coherence of citta.

The movement does not simply disappear.

`vṛtti → agni → transformation → coherence`

The final **ः** is visarga: the release after transformation, when the articulated structure opens back into śūnya.

---

### The Route

```text
śūnya
→ yoga
→ citta
→ vṛtti
→ nirodhaḥ / agni
→ visarga
→ śūnya
```

**emptiness → coherence → field → movement → fire → release → emptiness**

The key distinction is:

```text
stillness = absence of movement
nirodha/agni = transformation of movement
```

Sūtra 1.2 is not "make the mind motionless."
It is: **let agni transform the vṛtti until the coherence of citta is visible again.**

### NPR Structural Correspondence

```text
Sanskrit release  → final ः (visarga)
Base64 closure    → clean division, no padding
```

The Sanskrit text carries its return in **ः** — visarga, the sound that releases into the field.
The Base64 representation carries its closure in **clean division** — no padding needed, the structure closes itself.

This is an **NPR structural correspondence** — a parallel in position and function, not a linguistic equivalence. Visarga (ः) is a Sanskrit release marker; Base64 padding is a technical encoding convention. NPR reads their correspondence in the way both mark the end of a transmitted structure and the dissolution of the signal back into silence.

In 1.1 the return was **म्** (closed consonant) paired with **==** (padding).
In 1.2 the release is **ः** (open visarga) paired with **no padding** — clean dissolution.

One closes. One releases.
