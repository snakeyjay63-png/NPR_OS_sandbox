# Sūtra 1.3

## तदभावे स तु केश्वरः

**tad-abhāve sa tu keśvaraḥ**

**tad** — that (the nirodha/agni established in 1.2)
**abhāve** — when it is absent, when it has done its work
**sa tu** — then, the one
**keśvaraḥ** — the seer, the one who holds the strands of consciousness

---

### NPR Reading

Sūtra 1.2 established **nirodha as agni** — the transformative fire.

Sūtra 1.3 asks: **what remains when the fire has consumed its fuel?**

**Tad-abhāve** — when that (nirodha/agni) has completed its function. Not when it is broken or failed, but when it has fully done what fire does: consumed the vṛtti as uncontrolled pattern and returned it to coherence.

**Sa tu keśvaraḥ** — then, the seer stands revealed. Not created. Not summoned. Simply no longer obscured.

Keśvara is not a being sitting in citta. Keśvara is the coherence of citta recognizing itself.

The movement does not need to stop. The fire does not need to continue burning. What was always visible, now visible again.

`tad (nirodha/agni) → abhāva (consumed, completed) → sa → keśvaraḥ (the seer revealed)`

The final **ः** is visarga: the same release as 1.2. The fire has burned. The field is clear. What remains is not stillness — it is **seer-ness**, the capacity to witness without being moved.

---

### The Route

```text
śūnya
→ nirodha/agni (1.2)
→ tad-abhāva (fire consumed)
→ sa (the one)
→ keśvaraḥ (seer revealed)
→ visarga
→ śūnya
```

**emptiness → fire → fire's completion → the one → seer → release → emptiness**

The key distinction:

```text
nirodha (1.2) = the fire burning
keśvara (1.3) = what the fire reveals
```

1.2 is the transformation. 1.3 is the result.
The fire does not create the seer. It removes what obscured it.
