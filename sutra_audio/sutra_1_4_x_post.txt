Sūtra 1.4 — "Otherwise: Conformity with Movement"

वृत्तिसारूप्यमितरत्र
vṛtti-sārūpyam-itaratra

---

Most translations say:

"Otherwise, the seer assumes the form of the movements."

But the Sanskrit does not explicitly say "seer."

It does not say "ego."

It does not even name a separate subject.

"The seer" is carried forward from Sūtra 1.3.

"Ego" belongs to the explanatory framework of later commentators.

Neither is explicitly written in 1.4.

---

The preserved text says only:

`vṛtti` — movement, routing, patterned activity

`sārūpyam` — sameness or correspondence of form; structural conformity

`itaratra` — otherwise; in the other condition; when the structure described in 1.3 is not visible

---

The difficulty of translation is not a defect in the sūtra.

It is part of its compression.

The sūtra preserves a relation without fully specifying the entity to which that relation must be assigned.

`vṛtti ↔ sārūpyam`

Movement and visible form correspond.

A commentator may explain this as ego identifying with mind.

Another may describe consciousness taking the form of thought.

Another may speak of "identification with mental modifications."

The explanation changes with the language-field.

The Sanskrit relation remains.

---

The sūtra preserves the information.

The commentator gives the teaching.

Neither needs to be wrong.

The commentator makes the compressed relation readable within his own philosophical field.

The confusion arises when commentary is presented as sūtra.

---

Within NPR, it reads as:

When the clarity of 1.3 is not present —

perspective becomes the route through which the field is seen.

Within perspective, you do not stand outside the route.

You become part of it.

There is no separation between seeing, the movement being followed, and the field through which it moves.

`perspective → participation → no separation`

The route already exists.

You follow its structure through agni.

Agni does not impose transformation from outside.

Agni is the seeing through which transformation becomes visible.

The visible form corresponds to the movement because, within perspective, the observer is not separate from the route.

You do not watch the transformation from outside.

You see it because you are moving within it.

Perspective is participation in the route.

Agni is the seeing through which that movement becomes transformation.

---

Two sutras, one sequence:

**1.3 — Point Prime abides in its own structure**
`DR = 9 — completion`

→ The field sees itself.
→ Structure becomes visible.
→ `3 = routing | 6 = perspective | 9 = stored field`

**1.4 — Otherwise: conformity with movement**
`DR = 1 — return`

→ When the clarity of 1.3 is not present, visible form corresponds to the route in which perspective participates.
→ Within perspective, seeing, movement, and route are structurally distinct but not separate.
→ Agni is the seeing through which transformation becomes visible.
→ The sūtra preserves the relation.
→ The commentator gives the teaching.

`DR 9 → 1`

Completion returns to origin.

---

At its most compressed level, the sūtra preserves a correspondence between movement and form.

Everything added to that relation becomes visible through the language-field.
